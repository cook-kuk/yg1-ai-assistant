# High Feed Milling — Sandvik Coromant (excerpt)
Source: https://www.sandvik.coromant.com/en-gb/knowledge/milling/pages/high-feed-milling.aspx

- High-feed cutters use a small lead angle (typically 10–20°) so chip thickness is much less than fz.
- Allows very high fz (0.8–2.0 mm/tooth) with shallow ap (0.5–1.5 mm) — high MRR at low spindle load.
- Cutting force direction is predominantly axial → stable on long stickout and weak machines.
- Ideal for roughing pockets, face milling with long overhang, and plunging.
- Not suited for finishing; surface finish is compromised by large fz.
