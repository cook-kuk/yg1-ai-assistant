# Kennametal — Holemaking Tech Tips

## Reamer chatter
- Synchronized vibration of tool/workpiece/machine → deflection and poor finish.
- Feed rate should be 200–300% higher than drilling; Vc ~2/3 of drilling.
- Use uneven-flute reamer; rigid holder (shrink-fit/hydraulic) to minimize runout.

## Hole oversize (drilling / reaming)
- Pre-drill ~3% smaller than finish hole.
- Too much stock → chip packing, breakage; too little → rubbing, poor life/finish.
- Check runout <0.005 mm; use quality toolholders.

## Drill walking / misalignment
- Use spot drill or self-centering point geometry.
- Reduce feed at entry; ensure rigid setup.

## Tap breakage
- Too little chip clearance at bottom of blind hole.
- Use spiral-flute tap for blind holes, spiral-point for through.
- Check tapping depth, thread %, lubrication.

## Indexable drilling
- Insert chipping, long unbroken chips, chatter, oversize hole, fracturing, short life.
