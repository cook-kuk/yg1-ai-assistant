# ISO 513 — Material Groups in Machining

Source: https://www.machiningdoctor.com/glossary/material-groups-in-machining/
Fetched: 2026-04-09

ISO 513 defines six main workpiece material groups for metal cutting with defined cutting edges. Each main group has an identifying letter and a conventional color used by most carbide tool makers (Sandvik, Kennametal, Walter, Mitsubishi, etc.).

- P — Steel — BLUE
  Non-alloy, low/medium alloy, high alloy steels, cast steel, free-cutting steel. Long-chipping, moderate cutting forces. Most common group.

- M — Stainless Steel — YELLOW
  Austenitic, austenitic-ferritic (duplex), ferritic/martensitic stainless, cast stainless. High work-hardening, high cutting forces, built-up edge.

- K — Cast Iron — RED
  Grey cast iron (GCI), nodular (SG/ductile) iron, malleable iron, compacted graphite iron (CGI). Short-chipping, abrasive.

- N — Non-ferrous — GREEN
  Aluminum and aluminum alloys, copper and brass, bronze, magnesium, zinc, non-metals (plastics, composites). Low cutting forces, high speeds.

- S — Superalloys / Heat-resistant — BROWN (or orange)
  Nickel-based (Inconel, Hastelloy, Waspaloy), cobalt-based, iron-based superalloys, titanium alloys (Ti-6Al-4V). Very high temperature at the cutting edge, work-hardening.

- H — Hardened Materials — GREY (often shown as white)
  Hardened steel (48-65 HRC), chilled cast iron, hard-chrome, stellite. Hard turning / hard milling domain.

## Subgroup numbering (01..50)

Each main group is further split into application subgroups with a two-digit code. The rule of thumb:
- Lower numbers (e.g., P01, P10, K10, M10) = harder, more wear-resistant grade, for finishing, high speed, low feed, stable conditions.
- Higher numbers (e.g., P30, P40, K30, M30) = tougher grade, for roughing, interrupted cuts, lower speed, higher feed, unstable conditions.

Typical focus:
- P01 / P10 — finishing steel, high speed, fine finish
- P20 — general-purpose steel turning/milling
- P30 / P40 — roughing steel, interrupted cuts, tough grade
- M10 — finishing stainless
- M20 — general-purpose stainless
- M30 — roughing stainless / heavy duty
- K10 — finishing grey iron, abrasion resistance
- K20 — general-purpose cast iron
- K30 — roughing, interrupted cuts in cast iron
- N10 — finishing aluminum / non-ferrous
- N20 — general non-ferrous
- S10 — finishing superalloys / titanium
- S20 — general superalloy / titanium
- S30 — rough / interrupted HRSA
- H10 — finishing hardened steel (hard turning)
- H20 — general hard machining
